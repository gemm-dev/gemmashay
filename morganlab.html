Morgan Lab Group

About

While studying at Wesleyan, I had the opportunity to be involved in Morgan Lab Group's High Energy Molecular Spectroscopy reseach in a variety of capacities. I spent my first semester as a research assistant learning the experimental methods used in the lab, including high-vacuum technology, lasers, electronics, data analysis and computer modeling. In the spring of 2016, we attended the 2016 American Physical Society DAMOP conference in Providence, RI, where our research was presented.


The following summer, I received a fellowship from Wesleyan's Quantitative Analysis Center to participate in the Undergraduate Research in the Sciences summer program. During this time, I continued to assist in lab work while also completing a personal project simulating the orbits of Rydberg Helium, an atom of particular interest to our lab.
